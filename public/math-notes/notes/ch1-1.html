Chapter 1.1

Axiom 1.1
i) Commutativity of Addition: $m + n = n + m$.
ii) Associativity of Addition: $m + (n + p) = (m + n) + p$.
iii) Distributivity: $m \cdot (n + p) = (m \cdot n) + (m \cdot p)$.
iv) Commutativity of Multiplication: $m \cdot n = n \cdot m$.
v) Associativity of Multiplication: $m \cdot (n \cdot p) = (m \cdot n) \cdot p$.

Axiom 1.2, Identity Element for Addition: There exists and integer $0$ such that whenever $m \in \mathbb{Z}, m + 0 = m$.
Axiom 1.3, Identity Element for Multiplication: There exists and integer $1$ such that $1 \ne 0$ and $m \in \mathbb{Z}, m \cdot 1 = m$.
Axiom 1.4, Additive Inverse: For each $m \in \mathbb{Z}$, there exists an integer, denoted by $-m$, such that $m + (-m) = 0$.
Axiom 1.5, Cancellation: Let $m$, $n$, and $p$ be integers. If $m \cdot n = m \cdot p$, then $n = p$.
